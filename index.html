A short story about a librarian who discovers that books in her collection are mysteriously changing their contents, forcing her to question the nature of truth and permanence.

That changed on a Tuesday morning in October when she discovered that page 247 of "The Great Gatsby" no longer contained the famous green light passage...
Short Fiction

Letters to Tomorrow

A collection of interconnected vignettes told through letters written by people in the past to their future selves, exploring themes of hope, regret, and change.

The collection opens with Sarah, age sixteen, writing to herself at thirty: "Dear Future Sarah, I hope you remember what it feels like to believe that anything is possible. I hope you haven't forgotten how the air smells after rain, or how it feels to stay up all night talking about books with someone who really understands..."

The Weight of Empty Rooms

A meditation on loneliness and connection through the story of an apartment building where residents communicate only through notes left in common spaces.

Apartment 3B had been empty for six months when the first note appeared in the laundry room: "To whoever keeps leaving their clothes in the washer overnight—we all have schedules to keep. Please be considerate. -Management." But there was no management. The building had been self-managed since Mr. Chen, the superintendent, had passed away the previous spring...

Philosophical Reflections

On the Ethics of Attention

A philosophical inquiry into attention as a moral resource, examining our responsibilities in how we direct our focus in an age of infinite distraction.

In our hyperconnected age, attention has become perhaps our scarcest and most valuable resource. Yet we rarely consider the ethical dimensions of how we allocate this finite capacity. This reflection argues that attention is not merely a cognitive process but a moral practice that shapes both our character and our relationships with others...
Ethics

The Philosophy of Everyday Rituals

Exploring how daily practices and rituals create meaning, identity, and connection in both personal and communal contexts.

The morning coffee, the bedtime story, the weekly phone call to parents—our lives are structured by countless small rituals that we perform with minimal conscious reflection. Yet these seemingly mundane practices serve profound philosophical functions, creating continuity of identity, connecting us to communities, and anchoring meaning in the flow of time...
Philosophy

Uncertainty as Wisdom

An exploration of intellectual humility and the value of embracing uncertainty as a pathway to deeper understanding and empathy.

In an age of information abundance and polarized certainties, intellectual humility has become a radical position. This reflection argues that acknowledging the limits of our knowledge is not a weakness but the beginning of wisdom, and that uncertainty can be a source of empathy, creativity, and ethical growth...
